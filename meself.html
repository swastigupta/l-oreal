HANDLING AMBIGUITY AND CHAOS

"Clear communication and setting my priorities straight has been the best way to prevent ambiguity and chaos so far."

After being roommates for half a year with a college friend, I found out that she suffered from kleptomania. Missing a pair of earrings since 4 months, I looked for them in her wardrobe. To my utter shock, I found few other petty things of mine as well that I hadn’t even realized were missing. I had 3 options- complain to the hostel warden, confront her or take my things and change my room. Having bonded so well with her, I couldn’t convince myself to humiliate her publically in such a way but turning a blind eye wasn’t right either. So when she returned, I asked her if those earrings were hers. She declined. Then I told her where I had found it. I didn’t ask for any explanation. She cried silently for a while. We did not talk for weeks. All the noise that could be heard in our room was of switches being turned on or off. It took a long time but eventually, things turned normal. Today, we are best of friends and I am glad that in that moment I put my principles over and above my anger and shock and did not do anything which would have had a drastic impact.

OVERCOMING PERSONAL CONFLICTS

 In the 3rd year of college, I contested for the post of cultural secretary of student council. All my friends, juniors, seniors had assumed by default that I would win considering all the work I had done till then in all the college festivals and clubs. I felt confident about winning but got really nervous while presenting and lost the elections. Post that there was an air of tension between me and the contestant I lost to- sometimes owing to our conflicts of opinion and at times due to others banking on our communication gaps.

It was highly necessary for us to be on the same page to conduct the cultural festival smoothly. I had long one-to-one chats with her so as to minimize our conflicts and even explained the whole system of the cultural festival, what each category does and everything else. I felt that she caught up fast and did a great job. I won’t say that we became friends but we certainly worked out a way to work professionally towards a common goal.
